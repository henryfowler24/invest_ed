Workshop Challenge: The Community Capital Conundrum

Imagine Dover-Sherborn has been granted a **$1,000,000 endowment** to invest for the long-term benefit of the community. The goal is to generate sustainable returns that can fund local initiatives, scholarships, or infrastructure improvements, while also considering ethical and environmental factors.

**Your task:** Develop a comprehensive investment strategy for this endowment. Consider the following:

  • **Asset Allocation:** How would you diversify the $1,000,000 across different asset classes (e.g., stocks, bonds, real estate, alternative investments)? Justify your choices based on risk tolerance and return objectives.
  • **Ethical Considerations:** Are there any industries or companies you would actively avoid investing in (e.g., fossil fuels, tobacco, companies with poor labor practices)? How would you incorporate Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) principles into your strategy?
  • **Return Objectives:** What realistic annual return would you aim for, and how would this be used to benefit the community?
  • **Inflation Protection:** How would you protect the endowment's purchasing power over time?
  • **Community Involvement:** How might community members or local organizations have a say in how the funds are invested or how the returns are utilized?

Be prepared to present your strategy, defend your decisions, and outline potential risks and mitigation plans.
